Reusing is better than Recycling

Recycling Electronics might be good, but reusing them is better. Learn why reusing electronics is better than recycling electronics here.

E-waste Facts

E-Waste

The amount of e-waste being produced could rise by as \ much as 500% in developing countries like India, within the next ten years.

Nature

80 to 85% of all e-waste is disposed in landfills or incinerators, which releases toxins in the air and soil.

World

Each year, about 50 millions tons of E-waste are produced, and it keeps growing by 4% each year.

RRR

Only 13% of electronics are recycled each year, and 15% of computer users recycle them.

Electronics

The average cell phone user gets a new phone every 18 months. That is one phone in one and a half years!

Sales

Australians buy 4 million computers and 3 million television sets every year.

Recycling vs Reusing

The average American uses his or her phone for approximately 18 months, then discards it. And on top of that, only 40% of the E-waste are recycled, and of those 40%, only 70% of the electronics can actually be recovered. Even recycling electronic waste have consequences. However, there is a solution. By, instead of recycling unwanted electronics, using the electronics to their full potential, E-waste can be decreased. One's waste can be the other's treasure. Reusing the electronics not only helps the environment, but also can even help other people. This is Electroternity's goal, to protect the environment from E-waste and to give others an opprotunity of what it is like enjoying a phone.
