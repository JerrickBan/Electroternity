So what exactly is E-waste?

E-waste is the term used for electronics that serve no purpose anymore. Whether it's because the owner abandoned it or it just doesn't work anymore. Examples of E-waste include, TVs, phones, tablets, computer monitors, printers, MP3 and CD players. Many people who have disfunctional electronics quickly dispose of them. However, E-waste severely damages the environment and the earth we live on, which is a reason good enough to avoid it. Now on to the main reason this website was created: why in the world is E-waste bad?

How does E-waste Affect the Ecosystem?

Some ways that E-waste Hurt the Environment are through air, water, and soil pollution. Learn more about each of them down below.

Electronics in general contain dangerous chemicals. When E-waste arrive at landfills, many of them are burned or stripped. However, when they are burned or stripped they release hydrocarbons, brominated dioxins, and heavy metals.

When electronics are broken apart, materials like lead and barium can seep through the ground and enter the ground water. These elements are dangerous and can harm humans and animals that drink or bathe in it.

Particles can enter the water and then absorbed by the soil. This puts unhealthy elements into the soil and therefore can contaminate crops. If someone consumes the crop, he or she will get sick.




How does E-waste Affect Humans?

  1. Lead: damage to reproductive, blood, and nervous system
  2. Mercury: damage to brain and kidney
  3. Arsenic: damage skin, liver, nervous, and respiratory systems
  4. Cadmium: liver damage
  5. Chlorine: tissue damage
  6. Bromine: Brain damage and causes cancer
